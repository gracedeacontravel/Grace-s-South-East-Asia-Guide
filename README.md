        <h2>The Ultimate Guide to Solo Backpacking Southeast Asia at 20</h2>
        <div class="meta">By Grace • Updated Dec 2025 • 12 min read</div>
        <article>
          <p>Hi — I’m Grace. I flew out at 18 and spent two years backpacking around SE Asia on a shoestring. This guide condenses everything I learned so you can plan a confident, low-stress first solo trip.</p>

          <h3>1. Where to go first</h3>
          <p>Start with countries that have good transport links, cheap costs, and friendly hostels: <strong>Thailand, Vietnam, Cambodia</strong>. If you want beaches, add Thailand or Indonesia. If you want culture and cheap food, Vietnam and Cambodia are perfect.</p>

          <h3>2. How long to go</h3>
          <p>For your first solo backpacking trip, aim for <strong>3–6 weeks</strong>. This gives you time to adjust, recover from travel days, and explore without constantly moving. If your budget is tight, 2 weeks focusing on one country (e.g., Vietnam north-to-south) works well.</p>

          <h3>3. Budget (real numbers)</h3>
          <p>Typical daily budget ranges in 2025 (estimates):</p>
          <ul>
            <li><strong>Budget backpacker:</strong> £15–25/day — dorms, street food, public buses.</li>
            <li><strong>Comfort backpacker:</strong> £30–50/day — private rooms occasionally, a few guided tours.</li>
            <li><strong>Savings tips:</strong> cook some meals, use local buses, book hostels with kitchens, travel overnight on buses/trains.</li>
          </ul>

          <h3>4. Packing (short)</h3>
          <div class="packing-list" aria-hidden="false">
            <div class="packing-item">Light backpack (30–40L)</div>
            <div class="packing-item">Packable rain jacket</div>
            <div class="packing-item">Toiletries + quick-dry towel</div>
            <div class="packing-item">Phone + powerbank</div>
            <div class="packing-item">Copy of passport & emergency card</div>
          </div>
          <p style="margin-top:8px">Full packing list in the <a href="#packing">Packing & Gear</a> section (with affiliate links).</p>

          <h3>5. Safety tips for solo female travellers</h3>
          <ol>
            <li>Trust your instincts — if a place feels off, leave.</li>
            <li>Use female-only dorms if they make you feel safer.</li>
            <li>Share your location with a trusted friend on arrival days.</li>
            <li>Keep photocopies of important documents separate from originals.</li>
          </ol>

          <h3>6. Getting around</h3>
          <p>Buses, trains, cheap flights — use apps like Skyscanner for flights and local booking apps (Klook, 12Go). Overnight buses and trains save on accommodation costs.</p>

        <h2>Vietnam Backpacking Guide — Route, Budget, & Tips</h2>
        <div class="meta">By Grace • Vietnam</div>
        <article>
          <p>Vietnam was one of my favourite countries for budget travel. Here’s a 3-week route that works great for first-timers.</p>
          <h3>Sample 3-week route</h3>
          <ol>
            <li>Hanoi (3 days) — Old Quarter, street food</li>
            <li>Sapa (2 days) — easy homestays</li>
            <li>Halong Bay (1–2 days) — overnight cruise</li>
            <li>Hue & Hoi An (4 days) — culture and beaches</li>
            <li>Da Nang (1 day) — onward to central Vietnam</li>
            <li>Ho Chi Minh City (3–4 days) — busy southern hub</li>
          </ol>

          <h3>Budget example (mid 2025)</h3>
          <p>Expect to spend about <strong>£20–30/day</strong> if you sleep in dorms and eat street food. Use overnight buses to save accommodation costs.</p>

          <h3>Local scams to watch for</h3>
          <ul>
            <li>Taxi overcharging — use trusted apps like Grab where available</li>
            <li>Fake tour sellers near tourist spots — book from reputable sites or hostels</li>
          </ul>

    <div style="display:grid; gap:10px; grid-template-columns: repeat(auto-fit, minmax(240px,1fr))">
      <div>
        <h4>Meet people</h4>
        <p>Stay in social hostels, join walking tours, or use hostel common rooms to meet solo travellers.</p>
      </div>
      <div>
        <h4>Money</h4>
        <p>Keep a day’s cash separate. Use a travel card with no foreign transaction fees and withdraw larger amounts to avoid ATM fees.</p>
      </div>
      <div>
        <h4>Health</h4>
        <p>Drink bottled water when unsure, carry rehydration sachets, and know where the local clinic is in bigger towns.</p>
      </div>
      <div>
        <h4>Backups</h4>
        <p>Keep digital copies of passport and important documents in an encrypted cloud and one printed copy in a different bag.</p>
